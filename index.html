What you are looking at

The substrate on which cooperative life grows

Soil scientists read the past in layers. The deepest layer formed first, under the most pressure, and endures longest. Above it, progressively younger and more active strata accumulate — the mineral horizon where structure is set, then the organic layer where work and growth happen.

RegenHub's information system follows the same pattern. The bottom layer holds the bare primitives: who exists, what they agreed to, what they hold, what they did. The layers above it are more specific, more alive, and more subject to change. Strata is the field guide to those layers.

This site is a construction zone — honest about what is built, what is drafted, and what is planned. Status labels mean what they say.

⬡ Construction zone

The schema shown here is defined in migration files and under review. The Supabase instance has not yet been provisioned. When deployment occurs, live row counts and event log snapshots will appear here. Until then, this is the blueprint.

Architecture

Four layers, one system

The CIS is organized in layers, each resting on the one below it. Lower layers are more stable and more general. Higher layers are more specific and more easily evolved.

L1 · Primitives
The four primitives — REA ontology
Agents (who), Agreements (what they committed to), Resources (what they hold), Events (what they did). Every cooperative fact is an instance of one of these four primitives. This layer changes rarely. When it changes, it changes deliberately.

L2 · Audit
The unified event stream — append-only
Every state change, every access decision, every Nou interaction is written here. Nothing is deleted. The event log is simultaneously the economic record and the governance audit trail — one stream, readable as a continuous business narrative.

L3 · Policies
Row-level security — cite-as-you-enforce
Every policy carries a bylaw citation. A reviewer reading a policy reads the governance basis for it. When the bylaws change, the policy is a revision signal. RLS is the membrane — selective permeability enforced at the database level, not the application level.

Design principles

Ten commitments the system keeps

These are not aspirations. They are structural constraints — features of how the system is built, not policies adopted later.

01
Augmentation, not automation
The CIS makes members more capable, not less necessary. No decision is automated without a human in the loop.
02
Bylaws as specification
Every RLS policy cites the bylaw section that authorizes it. A bylaw amendment is a policy revision signal. The schema enforces what the bylaws say.
03
Default deny
No table is open by default. A row not matched by a permissive policy is invisible. Openness is granted explicitly, not assumed.
04
Append-only event log
Nothing is deleted from the audit log. Corrections are new events. The past is immutable. Triggers enforce this at the database layer — no policy or role can bypass it.
05
Soft deletes throughout
All records have an archived_at column. Deletion is an event (agent.archived), not an erasure. History is always recoverable.
06
Stable addresses for bylaws
Bylaw sections use kind:version:segment identifiers. A clause that moves in a new version retains its stable address. Citations survive amendments.
07
Legibility for all audiences
Every table has a plain-language description. Every policy comment names its governance basis. The database should be readable by a non-engineer board member.
08
Documented exit paths
Every vendor dependency (Supabase, GitHub, Mercury) has a documented walkaway path. Export views exist for all primitive tables. The system is not trapped in any one platform.
09
Privacy at the row level
Members cannot see each other's capital balances, resource records, or event history without explicit governance authorization. Privacy is structural, not procedural.
10
Composability over coupling
The REA primitive layer (L1) is stable and general. Modules (capital, hub ops, voting) extend it without modifying it. New layers do not break old ones.
